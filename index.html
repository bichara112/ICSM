Where complexity meets simplicity

Using mathematics to understand the fundamental principles driving organized systems, at a variety of scales: from neurons to human societies.

All around us there are systems composed of a large number of elements of identical or different nature, that interact with each other in a nonlinear way. If the overall behavior of the system cannot be determined or predicted by the behavior of each element taken seperately, then this system is said to be complex. The rules describing the interactions between the parts are of utmost importance; and thus echoing the famous quote "the whole is more than the sum of its parts". Patterns of wealth and income distribution, stock market fluctuations, population distributions between cities, and patterns of urban development are few examples of complex systems.

The world is changing at a breakneck pace and we are faced to meet the resulting challenges. Thus, the ability to describe in quantitative manners a natural phenomenon to gain insights on its possible behavior has become an increasingly important tool in modern society. We aim to develop mathematical tools and simulations to address these timely challenges.
